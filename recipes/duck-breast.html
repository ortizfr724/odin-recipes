Pan Seared Duck Breast

As the days grow shorter and the air turns crisp, there's no better way to indulge in the flavors of the season than with a Pan Seared Duck Breast. This rich and savory dish, paired with creamy mashed potatoes and a sweet-tart red cherry glaze, is the epitome of autumnal comfort food. To complement the robust flavors of the duck, a glass of full-bodied Shiraz offers a perfect pairing, with its dark fruit notes and velvety tannins enhancing the overall dining experience.

Ingredients

For the Duck Breast

  • 2 duck breast, skin on
  • Course sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Extra virgin olive oil

For the Mashed Potatoes

  • 2 pounds waxy potatoes, peeled and quartered
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 stick of butter, unsalted
  • Coarse sea salt and white pepper to taste

For the Red Cherry Glaze

  • 1 cup fresh or frozen cherries, pitted
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon water

Instructions

Prepare the Duck Breast

  1. Score the skin of the duck breast in a crosshatch pattern.
  2. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat, add a drizzle of olive oil.
  4. Place the duck breast skin-side down with a forward motion away from your body into the skillet. Cook skin-side down for 8-10 minutes or until the skin is crispy and the fat has rendered.
  5. Flip the duck breast over away from your body and cook for 3-4 minutes for medium to medium-well.
  6. Remove from the pan and let rest for 5 minutes before slicing.

Making the Mashed Potatoes

  1. Boil potatoes in salted water till tender.
  2. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot.
  3. In a small pot slowly heat milk and butter till warm.
  4. Mash the potatoes completely, slowly add warm milk and butter, whip together and season to taste.

Prepare the Red Cherry Glaze

  1. In a small sauce pot combine the cherries, sugar, balsamic vinegar together, combine the water and cornstarch together till there's no lumps, then add the cornstarch and water mixture to the sauce pot.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer until sauce thickens.

To Serve

  1. Place mashed potatoes in the center of your plate.
  2. Fan the duck slices to one side of the plate.
  3. Drizzle the cherry glaze over and in front of the slices.

Optional

  • If you prefer a well-done duck breast cook for an additional 2-3 minutes before removing meat to rest.
  • To add a touch additional flavor to the mashed potatoes you can add some herbs and lemon zest.
