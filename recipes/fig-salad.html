Roasted Fig Salad

As the crisp days of fall begin to paint the landscape with vibrant hues, there's no better way to savor the season than with a Roasted Fig Salad paired with a lightly chilled white Chardonnay. This delightful combination marries sweet, caramelized notes of roasted figs with the refreshing acidity of the wine, creating a harmonious symphony of flavors that perfectly captures the essence of autumn.

Ingredients

  • 4 ripe figs
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon raw honey
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 1/2 cups mesclun mix
  • 1/4 cup toasted walnuts
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/8 teaspoon cracked pepper
    • use pepper-mill before serving salad

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare figs: Cut figs in half lengthwise. Place them cut side up on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Drizzle with balsamic vinegar, honey, and olive oil. Sprinkle with salt.
  3. Roast figs: Roast in preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until slightly caramelized.
  4. Assemble salad: In a large bowl, combine mesclun mix, walnuts, and feta cheese.
  5. Plate and serve: Divide the salad between two plates. Top with the roasted figs. Drizzle with any remaining balsamic glaze from the baking sheet. Serve immediately.
    6. Optional: You can add a drizzle of balsamic glaze or a squeeze of lemon juice for extra flavor.
